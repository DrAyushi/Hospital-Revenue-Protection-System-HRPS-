# Hospital Revenue Protection System (HRPS)

## Transforming Hospital Finance Through Intelligent Revenue Protection

The Hospital Revenue Protection System (HRPS) is a healthcare finance analytics initiative developed to address one of the most critical operational challenges in hospitals — revenue leakage caused by deduction losses, delayed payments, underpayments, and inefficient follow-up management.

Designed during a Summer Internship in the Finance Department at Kokilaben Dhirubhai Ambani Hospital, this project focuses on strengthening financial sustainability through process optimization, deduction visibility, and data-driven revenue recovery mechanisms.

---

## Executive Summary

Modern hospitals handle large-scale transactions involving TPAs, insurance providers, corporates, and self-paying patients. Despite strong operational systems, revenue losses still occur due to:

- Payment deductions without clear visibility
- Delayed corporate settlements
- Manual reconciliation dependency
- Missed or delayed follow-ups
- Lack of centralized deduction tracking

HRPS was conceptualized to support finance teams by creating a structured framework for monitoring, identifying, prioritizing, and recovering revenue leakages efficiently.

---

## Project Objectives

- Minimize hospital revenue leakage
- Improve deduction tracking and visibility
- Strengthen follow-up management systems
- Reduce manual finance workload
- Improve collection efficiency
- Enable smarter finance decision-making
- Support operational transparency

---

## Key Features

### Revenue Leakage Identification
Tracks gaps between billed amounts and received payments across TPAs and corporates.

### Deduction Analysis System
Helps identify deduction patterns, pending claims, and payment discrepancies.

### Follow-Up Tracking
Creates a structured workflow for pending recovery cases and delayed payments.

### Risk Prioritization Framework
Highlights high-risk accounts requiring immediate finance intervention.

### Operational Analytics
Provides insights into collection delays, deduction trends, and recovery opportunities.

## Existing Strengths Observed

Before identifying gaps, the project acknowledges the strong systems already established within the hospital:

- Well-structured billing infrastructure
- Experienced finance and reconciliation teams
- Strong operational discipline
- Organized patient billing workflows
- Efficient claim processing foundation

HRPS is designed to enhance these existing strengths through analytics and intelligent process support.
