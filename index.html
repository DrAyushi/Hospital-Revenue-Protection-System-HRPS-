Current Strengths & Observations

Building on Strong Foundations

Before discussing the challenges, I would first like to acknowledge the strong operational systems already existing within the Finance Department at Kokilaben Dhirubhai Ambani Hospital.

Well-structured billing and discharge workflows

Strong coordination between TPA, corporate, and finance teams

Efficient handling of large patient volumes

Detailed documentation and claim processes

Strong recovery efforts through manual follow-ups

Existing SAP and reporting support systems

High operational discipline in financial processes

Experienced finance team with domain expertise

However, due to the scale and complexity of corporate and TPA collections, many processes still depend heavily on manual tracking, follow-ups, and Excel-based monitoring.

This creates opportunities where automation, analytics, and intelligent tracking systems can further strengthen revenue protection and recovery efficiency.

The objective of HRPS is not to replace existing systems, but to enhance them through data-driven decision support and workflow automation.

The Problem

Revenue Leakage Nobody Is Tracking

Three critical gaps in hospital finance collections that cost crores every year

No Pre-Discharge Risk Flagging

Corporate patients are discharged without any assessment of whether their company will pay the full bill. High-risk companies like government PSUs and aviation companies routinely short-pay or dispute bills — after the patient has already left.

68% of losses come from just 5 companies

Completely Manual Follow-up

After discharge, TPA and corporate follow-ups are done manually — phone calls, emails, spreadsheets. No system tracks who owes what, for how long, and what action is overdue today.

32.5 days average payment delay

Deductions Never Recovered

TPA/Corporate pays less than billed. Nobody knows why automatically. Finance team manually finds bills, compares amounts, calls TPA. This takes weeks. Most deductions are never recovered — hospital silently loses money.

Module 1

The Risk Scoring Engine

5 Weighted Parameters

Payment Ratio30%
Avg Pay Delay25%
Outstanding20%
Dispute Count15%
Dispute Rate10%
Risk Score =
(1 - pay_ratio) × 30
+ (avg_days / 130) × 25
+ (outstanding / 50) × 20
+ (disputes / 10) × 15
+ (dispute_rate) × 10

Risk Level Output

HIGH RISK — Score 70 to 100

Action: Collect advance before discharge

Companies: Air India, Western Railway, AAI

MEDIUM RISK — Score 40 to 69

Action: Verify LOG amount and monitor

Companies: BARC, IDBI, BPCL

LOW RISK — Score 0 to 39

Action: Proceed normally

Companies: TCS, HDFC Bank, Wipro
